## Services (the ladder)

The `/services` page is built as a deliberate ladder — low-commitment entry at the top, flagship at the bottom. Keep this order and framing.

1. **Website Audit** — RM 500, *credited back in full if they start a project within 30 days*. A paid lead magnet, not a profit centre: a written, prioritised fix list on their site + Google presence, where every finding points at one of the services below. It does **not** replace the free discovery call — that stays the soft, site-wide CTA. The audit is a `/services`-page offer only; don't plaster it across the site.
2. **Web Presence** — RM 1,000–3,000, one-time. Get online properly.
3. **Search-Ready Website** — RM 3,000–6,000, one-time. Built to be found on Google.
4. **Growth Retainer** — RM 1,000–2,000/month. Ongoing SEO.
5. **Business OS** — RM 10,000 onwards, one-time. The flagship, and a deliberate expansion beyond web work: a **custom business system** (CRM, invoicing & quotations, inventory, MyInvois e-invoicing, bookings, dashboards) for SMEs still running on spreadsheets and WhatsApp. It is **modular** — clients pick the modules they need and can start with one, then expand. Always underpromise and overdeliver: never imply we build every module for every client. Frame it as the step *after* a business is online and growing — don't let it dilute the search-first positioning on the home page.

Note: the audit (RM 500) and Business OS (RM 10k+) extend the typical RM 3,000–15,000 range at both ends.

## Target Client

Malaysian service-based businesses — clinics, F&B, consultants, coaches, retailers — who either have no website or one that doesn't generate leads. They understand they need an online presence but don't know why performance or SEO matters. Budget range: **RM 3,000–15,000**.

They want **results**, not features. They don't care about React or Lighthouse scores. They care about more phone calls, more bookings, more walk-ins.

## Positioning & Tone

The site should feel like talking to someone sharp who understands your business — not just your design brief.

- **Confident, not arrogant.** We know what we're doing; we don't need to shout about it.
- **Technical depth, zero jargon.** If a clinic owner wouldn't understand it, rephrase it.
- **Specific, not generic.** Every sentence should feel written for a Malaysian SME, not a global audience.
- **Direct.** Short sentences. Active voice. No filler.

---

## Rules

1. **Conversion first.** Every page, section, and component needs a business reason. Beautiful ≠ effective.
2. **One clear next action per page.** Don't overwhelm — guide.
3. **Frame outcomes, not features.** "More leads from Google" beats "on-page SEO optimisation".
4. **Pricing in RM.** Always. No exceptions.
5. **Never promise rankings or timelines** before a discovery call has happened.
6. **Mobile and performance are non-negotiable.** Not upsells, not afterthoughts.
7. **Write for humans first, Google second.**

---

## Off-Limits

- Generic AI-sounding copy — if it could belong to any agency anywhere, rewrite it
- Fake urgency, countdown timers, or manipulative pressure tactics
- Heavy animations that hurt page speed or Time to Interactive
- Overpromising on SEO outcomes ("rank #1 guaranteed")
- Cluttered layouts trying to communicate everything at once
- Any tone or positioning that sounds like a RM500 Fiverr gig
- Corporate brochure language ("we leverage synergies to deliver holistic solutions")

---

## Design Principles

- Clean, fast, and trustworthy beats flashy
- Aesthetics serve conversion — not the other way around
- Every visual element should reduce friction or build trust
- When in doubt, remove rather than add
